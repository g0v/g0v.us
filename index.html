g0v is a civic tech community in Taiwan that advocates transparency of information and provides tech solutions to civic problems.

We are passionate coders, designers, activists, educators, writers and citizens.

We believe that a better society requires open data, open government and a open culture that brings everyone together.

g0v Manifesto

Freedom of Speech and Information Transparency

We aim to use technology in the interest of the public good, allowing citizens easy access to vital information.
Open Data
Opening up and making data public allows the people to take a closer look at politics and important issues.
Public Tools
We build tools that empower people to evaluate their government and exert their democratic right to decide how politicians act.

Independent & Transparent

Our areas of interest are broad. We have projects focusing on online deliberation, open congress, open budget, legal system, gender equality, healthcare, language and culture, environment issues, disaster management, etc.

g0v is a community of nobodies

We have a thousand and more contributors, including 40% coders, 10% designers, 20% NGOs workers and government servants, and 30% people from all walks of life. Until mid-2016, we have held 22 bi-monthly hackathons, 2 summits and regular meetups.

Our summit is one-of-the-kind event that invites international civic hackers to exchange innovative ideas and civic tech solutions with our local hackers.

Hackathon Management Team & Summit Team

Our community is grassroots.

We are non-partisan, unbiased and not for sale. Participants decide what to work on and are at the heart of our communal culture.

You can contribute to our hackathons with your skills, ideas, time or donations, or provide support for specific projects.

Our Projects

Government Budget Visualization

Supercharge citizens with the power of visualization and social network to supervise government spending.

Provide different perspectives of budget data like historical trend, cross-department comparison, public opinion and component breakdown of tax by government spending with interactive treemap, bubble chart and feedback mechanism.

Repository Website

Moedict

As an improved version of Ministry of Education's online Dictionary at the beginning, Moedict has became the most popular online dictionary with million pages views per month and 100k mobile apps users.

It now contains Mandarin / Taiwanese / Hakka / English / French / Germany languages and information like character stroke order and pronounciation.

Repository Website

Open Political Contributions

In Taiwan, political contributions data is only availabel in paper format. To analyze with computer, this project condcuted Crowdsourcing OCR to 2637 pages of control yuan's non-digitized version of political contributions data and make it open.

Total 309k entries are translated into organized data and web API within 24 hours.

Repository Website

Legislator Voting Guide

Legislator Voting Guide helps citizens gain more information about what legislators did in the past with quantitative approach and visualizations.

Legislators are sorted in various charts according to their records of absence, amount of legal proposal, etc. Their election platform and background are also provided for a cross reference.

Repository Website

LSL Calculator

Calculate amount of official holidays, minimum salary and maximum overtime hours per week based on different proposed modification of LSL ( Labor Standards Law ) through an unified interface.

Labors can know better about their overtime pay and holiday compensation under different proposed modification of LSL to understand and review the impact of them.
